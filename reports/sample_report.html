Market Overview

  • Funding Environment: Total funding down 40% YoY to $2.9B, but deal count relatively stable at 137 (vs 152 in 2023). Average deal size dropped from $30M to $21M.
  • Valuation Compression: Nigerian fintech now trades at 8x revenue (down from 12x in 2023), representing a 35-40% discount to US fintech benchmarks.

Investment Implications

For VCs: Exercise extreme selectivity. Focus on capital-efficient companies with clear paths to profitability. Distressed assets will create acquisition opportunities in H1 2025.

For Banks: Partnership risk is elevated. Thoroughly assess runway and burn rates before committing to integration projects or co-branded products.

For Corporates: M&A opportunities are emerging as valuations compress. Target companies with strong product-market fit but weak balance sheets.

Methodology

Our intelligence is built on rigorous data collection, multi-source verification, and investment-grade financial analysis.

Data Collection

Primary Sources
  • Company websites & investor relations: Funding announcements, press releases, official metrics
  • Regulatory filings: SEC, CAC, CBN filings where available
  • LinkedIn profiles: Team size, growth rate, key hires
  • App store data: Download counts, ratings, review analysis
Secondary Sources
  • Industry publications: TechCabal, Techpoint, Disrupt Africa, Quartz Africa
  • Funding databases: Crunchbase, PitchBook, Dealroom
  • Social media: Twitter, LinkedIn for company updates and sentiment
Primary Research
  • Founder interviews: 30-40 interviews per report cycle
  • Investor conversations: Direct input from active VCs
  • Industry expert consultations: Operators, advisors, ecosystem players

Verification Process

Every data point goes through our three-source verification rule:

  1. Initial collection: Data gathered from public sources
  2. Cross-reference: Validate against at least 2 additional independent sources
  3. Confidence scoring: Assign confidence level (High/Medium/Low) based on source quality
  4. Flagging: Uncertain or conflicting data points are clearly marked in reports
Data Quality Commitment: We prioritize accuracy over completeness. If we cannot verify a metric with high confidence, we explicitly state "Data unavailable" rather than publishing unverified estimates.
